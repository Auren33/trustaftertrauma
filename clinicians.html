Why This Matters

Betrayal trauma is one of the most common presenting issues in therapy — and one of the least trained.

Most therapy training programs spend hours on depression, anxiety, and OCD. Betrayal trauma — affecting tens of millions of partners, family members, and survivors — gets a paragraph, if that.

The Compass Recovery Model gives you a structured, evidence-informed framework for this work. Not just theory — a complete clinical map your clients can follow, stage by stage.

  • A proprietary 5-stage framework mapped to trauma recovery timelines
  • Stage-specific assessment tools to use with clients in session
  • Curriculum you can license and use in your own practice
  • Listing in the Compass Clinician Directory — referred clients find you
  • Access to a growing network of Compass-trained clinicians
  • CE credit hours (application submitted)
  • Academic foundation: doctoral-level research validation in progress
The Framework

The Five Stages of the Compass Recovery Model

Each stage maps to a specific phase of betrayal trauma recovery — with corresponding clinical interventions, assessment criteria, and client tools.

Center
Discovery Shock
The ICU Phase. Days to weeks. Priority: survival and stabilization.
North
Stabilization
Months 1–3. Nervous system regulation. Finding footing.
East
Integration
Making meaning. Deciding what the betrayal means for identity and relationship.
South
Healing
Rebuilding trust, intimacy, and self-relationship — whether staying or leaving.
West
Beyond Betrayal
Year 1+. Transformation. A new chapter with a rebuilt sense of self.

Built on Betrayal Trauma Theory (Freyd), EFT (Johnson), somatic therapy (Levine, van der Kolk), and CBT-based affair recovery frameworks (Snyder & Gordon). Validated through doctoral research at MSTI. Explore the full model →

Questions

Frequently Asked

Do I need to be a licensed therapist?

Level 1 and Level 2 are open to licensed mental health professionals, registered associates, certified coaches, and doctoral students. Level 3 (Certified) requires an independent clinical license and supervised clinical hours.

What are the CE credit hours?

CE credit application is currently submitted. We anticipate approval for Level 1 (12 CE hours) and Level 2 (24 additional CE hours). You will be notified of approval status at enrollment.

When does the founding cohort start?

Cohort 1 begins May 2026 (exact date confirmed at enrollment). Training is virtual, live, and cohort-based. Sessions are recorded for review but live attendance is expected.

Can I use the curriculum with my own clients?

Level 1 includes clinical tools (assessment, session templates) for use with individual clients. The Group Facilitator License (included in Level 2 and Level 3) allows you to run licensed Compass Recovery Groups in your practice.

What's the difference between the Facilitator License and full certification?

The Facilitator License allows you to run Compass groups using the curriculum. It's included in Level 2 and Level 3 pricing, and also available as a standalone renewal for $750/year. Certification (Levels 1–3) is the full clinical training pathway with designation and directory listing.

Are payment plans available?

Yes. Level 1: 3 × $550. Level 2: 3 × $1,250. Level 3: 5 × $1,100. Payment plans are processed via Stripe and must be active at the start of training. Contact us to arrange a custom plan.
